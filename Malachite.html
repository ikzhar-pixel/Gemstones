Malachite Characteristics

Hardness

Malachite is a soft mineral with a hardness of 3.5 to 4 on the Mohs scale. It can be easily scratched, so it needs gentle care

Transparency

Malachite is usually opaque, meaning you can’t see through it. Some very thin pieces might be slightly translucent at the edges

Sparkle

It doesn’t sparkle like diamonds or rubies. Instead, it has a silky or dull luster, but polished malachite can look smooth and shiny

Color

Malachite is known for its rich green color, often with beautiful light and dark green bands or swirls. No other stone looks quite like it!

Clarity

Since it’s opaque, clarity isn’t as important. What matters most is the pattern and intensity of the green color

Origin

Malachite forms near copper deposits, often in caves or cracks in rocks. It’s mined in places like the Australia, and the southwestern USA

About Malachite

Some Info

Malachite is a bright green gemstone known for its unique banded patterns. It has been used for thousands of years in jewelry, decorations, and even ancient art. Although it’s not very hard, people love it for its beautiful color and natural designs. Malachite is also believed to have protective and healing powers in many cultures.
