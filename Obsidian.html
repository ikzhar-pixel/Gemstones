About Obsidian

Black volcanic glass. It has sharp edges, so it was once used for tools

Obsidian Characteristics

Hardness

Obsidian has a hardness of about 5–5.5 on the Mohs scale. It’s not very hard and can be scratched by metal or glass

Transparency

Obsidian is usually opaque, though sometimes it can be slightly translucent at the edges, especially in thin pieces

Sparkle

It has a glassy luster, but it doesn’t sparkle like gemstones. Some types show a pearly or metallic sheen

Color

Most commonly black, but it can also appear brown, green, or even rainbow-colored
(called rainbow obsidian)

Clarity

Since it’s volcanic glass, obsidian doesn’t have a crystal structure. However, it may contain tiny gas bubbles or layers

Origin

Obsidian forms when lava rich in silica cools very quickly. It can be found in volcanic areas like the USA, Mexico, Iceland

About Obsidian

Some Info

Obsidian is a natural volcanic glass formed when lava cools very quickly. It’s usually black and shiny, with a smooth texture. People have used it for thousands of years to make tools, weapons, and jewelry. Though not a gemstone, it's popular for its mysterious look and sharp edges.
