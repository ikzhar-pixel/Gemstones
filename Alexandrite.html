About Alexandrite

This stone changes color: green in daylight, and violet or red under lamp light

Alexandrite Characteristics

Hardness

Alexandrite is quite hard — 8.5 on the Mohs scale. It’s durable enough for jewelry but softer than ruby or diamond

Transparency

Most alexandrites are transparent, though some may have slight cloudiness. Clean, clear stones are especially valuable

Sparkle

It has a bright, glass-like shine (vitreous luster). When well-cut, it reflects light beautifully

Color

This gem is famous for color change: green or bluish-green in daylight and reddish-purple under artificial light

Clarity

Top-quality alexandrites have very few inclusions. Clear stones with a strong color change are rare and highly prized

Origin

Alexandrite mined in countries like Brazil, Sri Lanka, India, Tanzania, Madagascar and Zimbabwe

About Alexandrite

Some Info

Alexandrite is a rare gemstone known for its ability to change color depending on the lighting. In daylight, it appears greenish-blue, and under artificial light, it turns reddish-purple. This effect is called "color change." Alexandrite is highly valued for its uniqueness and its high hardness (8.5 on the Mohs scale), making it ideal for use in jewelry.
