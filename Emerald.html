About Emerald

Emerald is one of the most famous and beautiful gemstones with a bright green color. It is valued for its depth of color

Emerald Characteristics

Hardness

Emerald has a hardness of 7.5–8 on the Mohs scale. It's quite hard, but more fragile than ruby or diamond due to natural inclusions

Transparency

Emeralds can be transparent to slightly cloudy. The clearer the emerald, the valuable it is — though most have visible inclusions

Sparkle

Emeralds have a soft, glassy luster. They don’t shine as brightly as diamonds, but their rich color gives them a deep glow

Color

Emeralds are always green — from light green to deep, rich green. The most valuable ones are intense, vivid green with a slight blue tone

Clarity

Most emeralds have inclusions or tiny cracks, known as the “jardin” (French for garden). Perfectly clear emeralds are extremely rare and very expensive

Origin

Emeralds form under special conditions in the Earth’s crust. They are mainly mined in countries like Colombia, Zambia, Brazil, and Afghanistan

About Emerald

Some Info

Emerald is a precious gemstone with a rich green color. It symbolizes beauty, youth, and harmony. Emeralds are valued for their vibrant color, although almost all of them have small cracks or inclusions. The most famous emerald deposits are in Colombia.
