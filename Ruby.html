About Ruby

Ruby is one of the most valuable gemstones. Its rich red color symbolizes love, energy, and power

Ruby Characteristics

Hardness

Ruby is one of the hardest gemstones — it scores 9 on the Mohs scale. It's very durable and can scratch most other minerals, except diamond

Transparency

Rubies can range from transparent to slightly translucent. Transparent rubies allow light to pass through clearly, showing off their color and sparkle

Sparkle

Rubies have a vitreous (glassy) luster, which means they shine like glass when light hits them. A well-cut ruby reflects light beautifully, especially on its polished surfaces

Color

Ruby’s color is its most important and valuable feature. Rubies come in shades of red, ranging from light pinkish-red to deep, dark red

Clarity

High-quality rubies usually have some natural inclusions (tiny crystals, lines, or spots inside the stone). These are normal and prove that the ruby is natural

Origin

Rubies form under high pressure and heat deep in the Earth. They are mined in countries like Myanmar (Burma), Thailand, Sri Lanka, and Mozambique

About Ruby

Some Info

Ruby is a precious red gemstone known for its beauty and strength. It ranks 9 on the Mohs scale, making it one of the hardest minerals. Rubies shine with a glassy luster and glow with a deep red color, often called “pigeon’s blood” when it’s especially vivid. They are symbols of passion, love, and power, and are found in places like Myanmar, Thailand, and Mozambique.
