About Amethyst

A purple variety of quartz. A symbol of calm, wisdom, and protection

Amethyst Characteristics

Hardness

Amethyst has a hardness of 7 on the Mohs scale. It's fairly durable and resistant to scratches, but softer than sapphire

Transparency

Most amethysts are transparent to translucent. The more transparent and clean the stone is, the higher its quality

Sparkle

Amethyst has a gentle, glassy shine. It’s not as sparkly as diamonds, but still looks beautiful in the light

Color

Amethyst is known for its purple shades — from light lilac to deep violet. The most valuable ones are dark and rich purple

Clarity

High-quality amethysts have very few or no visible inclusions. Clear stones with rich color are considered the best

Origin

Amethysts are found in many parts of the world, including Brazil, Uruguay, and Zambia. They form in volcanic rocks and geodes

About Amethyst

Some Info

Amethyst is a purple variety of quartz, known for its beautiful color and the calm it symbolizes. It has long been considered a stone of wisdom, protection, and inner harmony. Due to its beauty and availability, amethyst is one of the most popular semi-precious stones in the world.
