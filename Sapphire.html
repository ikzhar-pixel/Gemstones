Sapphire Characteristics

Hardness

Sapphire is very hard — 9 on the Mohs scale, just like ruby. It's one of the most durable gemstones, great for jewelry

Transparency

Sapphires are usually transparent, but some can be slightly cloudy. The more transparent the sapphire, the higher its value

Sparkle

Sapphires have a strong, glass-like shine called vitreous luster. When a sapphire is well-cut, it creates a bright, eye-catching sparkle

Color

Sapphires are best known for their deep blue color, but they also come in pink, yellow, green, white, and even orange. The only color they don’t come in is red (red sapphires are called rubies)

Clarity

Sapphires often have natural inclusions — tiny crystals, small fractures, or “clouds” inside the stone. This is normal and not always a bad thing. Some inclusions even help experts identify a sapphire as natural

Origin

Sapphires form deep within the Earth’s crust under intense heat and pressure, usually in areas with volcanic or metamorphic rocks. If aluminum and oxygen will combine, it will create corundum, from sapphire has made

About Sapphire

Some Info

Sapphire is a precious gemstone known for its deep blue color, though it can also be found in many other colors. It is very hard and durable, scoring 9 on the Mohs scale. Sapphires are often used in jewelry and are mined in countries like Sri Lanka, Myanmar, and Madagascar.
